Fertilizer & Pest Advisory
×

Pest & Fertilizer Guidance (2025)

  • Rice: 90–120N, 40–60P, 40–60K per ha. Split 1/2 basal, 1/4 at tillering, 1/4 panicle stage; monitor leaf folder, blast, sheath blight, stem borer.
  • Wheat: 120–150N, 60P, 40K per ha; top-dress after irrigation; watch rusts, aphids, armyworm.
  • Cotton: 60–90N, 30–45P, 30–45K in 2–3 splits; beware of bollworm, jassid, whitefly.
  • Soybean: 30–40N, 60–80P, 30–40K per ha; healthy nodulation by rhizobium; monitor pod borers.
  • Vegetables (e.g., Tomato): 100–120N, 60–80P, 60–80K per ha plus Ca, B, organic matter if possible.
  • Pulse Crops: 20N starter, 40–60P, watch borers.

Autonomous Electric Tractor

This fully electric, driverless tractor uses advanced GPS and sensor technology to plow, plant, and spray with high precision, operating 24/7. It reduces labor costs and increases fuel efficiency.

How to Use
  • Define field boundaries using the companion app.
  • Select the task (e.g., plowing, seeding) and configure parameters like depth and speed.

Agricultural Drones (Agri-Drones)

Equipped with multispectral sensors, Agri-Drones provide invaluable data on crop health, soil conditions, and hydration levels. They are also used for precise pesticide and fertilizer spraying.

How to Use
  • Plan the flight path over your fields using the control software.
  • Launch the drone to automatically collect imagery and data.
  • Analyze the data maps to identify problem areas (e.g., pests, nutrient deficiencies).
  • Attach the sprayer module for targeted application of treatments, minimizing waste.
