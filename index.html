Major Indian Government Schemes for Seed Distribution

       
             
  • National Food Security Mission (NFSM): Subsidized certified seeds for staples like rice, wheat, pulses, reducing import needs.
    •          
  • Sub-Mission on Seeds and Planting Material (SMSP): Quality foundation & certified seeds, fosters public-private partnerships.
    •          
  • Rashtriya Krishi Vikas Yojana (RKVY): Funds for distributing improved, climate-adaptive seeds via mini-kits/local programs.
    •          
  • State-level Schemes: Beej Swalamban Yojana (Rajasthan), Seed Village Scheme (Maharashtra), regional mini-kits.
    •          
  • PM Dhan-Dhaanya Krishi Yojana (2025): Distributes high-quality seeds to under-yielding districts.
    •        
       

Subsidy Structure by Crop Type

       
             
  • Cereals: 50–60% subsidy, some free mini-kits (rice, wheat, maize, barley, millets)
    •          
  • Pulses: 60–75%, many mini-kits free (gram, lentil, moong, urad, pea)
    •          
  • Oilseeds: 60–75%, nominal/free for campaigns (mustard, sunflower, soybean, groundnut)
    •          
  • Vegetables & Fruits: Up to 60–75%, varies widely; kitchen garden kits sometimes free
    •          
  • Fodder/Green Manure: 60–75% on maize, sorghum, barseem, napier grass, cotton, jute
    •        
       

Special Features

       
             
  • Direct Benefit Transfer (DBT): Now most seed/farm subsidies are paid direct to bank accounts.
    •          
  • Hybrid Seed Promotion: Big push for hybrids in vegetable, oilseed, cereal crops (higher yield).
    •          
  • Climate Resilient Varieties: Targeting rainfed and challenging climates for long-term stability.

Major Govt Schemes for Farmers (2025)

       
             
  • PM-KISAN Samman Nidhi: ₹6000/yr in 3 installments to small & marginal farmers, direct to bank account.
    •          
  • Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana (PMFBY): Crop insurance for 50+ crops; premium 1.5–2% for food crops, 5% for horticulture.
    •          
  • Kisan Credit Card (KCC): Flexible, low-interest crop loans up to ₹3 lakh.
    •          
  • PM Krishi Sinchai Yojana (PMKSY): Subsidizes micro/drip irrigation systems & expands irrigation area.
    •          
  • Soil Health Card: Custom nutrient recommendations for higher yield.
    •          
  • RKVY-RAFTAAR: Grants for infra, input subsidies, agri innovation.
    •          
  • e-NAM: National Agri-Market for transparent crop selling, pan-India.
    •          
  • Paramparagat Krishi Vikas Yojana (PKVY): Supports organic farming clusters, input subsidies.
    •          
  • Agriculture Infrastructure Fund (AIF): Grants for storage, cold chain, processing/post-harvest units.
    •          
  • PM Matsya Sampada Yojana: Infrastructure for fisheries & aquaculture.
    •          
  • SMAM Mechanization: Farm machinery/tractor subsidy focused on marginal farmers.
    •        
       

Loan & Insurance Schemes

       
             
  • Crop/Weather Insurance: PMFBY (flagship), WBCIS (weather-based), state schemes.
    •          
  • Kisan Credit Card (KCC): Crop loans up to ₹3 lakh at 4% (prompt). Collateral-free for smallholders.
    •          
  • MSME/NABARD Loans: Short/long-term credit for agri-infra, solar pumps, FPOs, MSMEs.
    •          
  • Agriculture Infra Fund: Grants/loans for post-harvest units, cold storage, and machinery.
    •        
       

How to Apply

       

Most schemes available through banks, PACS, co-op societies, and official portals. KCC-loan holders are usually pre-enrolled for insurance. See website or local office for docs required.

Crop Calendar by Season, Region & Crop

       
             
  • Kharif (June–Sep): Paddy rice, maize, millets, cotton, soybean, groundnut, pulses, vegetables.
    •          
  • Rabi (Oct–Feb): Wheat, barley, oats, chickpea, mustard, pea, lentil, carrot, potato, onion, leafy greens.
    •          
  • Zaid (Mar–May): Watermelon, muskmelon, cucumber, summer moong, cowpea, quick vegetables.
    •        
       

Major Region Guide

       
             
  • North India: Kharif: Paddy, maize, sugarcane. Rabi: Wheat, barley, gram, pea, mustard.
    •          
  • South India: Kharif: Paddy, ragi, cotton, sunflower. Rabi: Ragi, groundnut, urad, chillies.
    •          
  • West: Cotton, soybean, groundnut (K); Wheat, barley, gram, cumin, mustard (R).
    •          
  • East/NE: Paddy, jute, maize, tea (K); Wheat, lentil, potato, vegetables (R). Zaid: Melons, pulses.
    •          
  • Himalayan: Maize, paddy, apple, barley, off-season veg (K/R, as per climate).
    •        
       

General Guide by Crop

       
             
  1. Rice: Kharif—nursery mid-Jun, transplant July. Harvest Oct–Nov. Maintain standing water especially early growth; 90–120 kg N, 40–60 kg P, 40–60 kg K per ha (split dosing).
    2.          
  2. Wheat: Rabi—sowing late Oct to mid Nov. 4–5 irrigations. 120–150 kg N, 60 kg P, 40 kg K. Harvest March–April.
    3.          
  3. Maize: Kharif (Jun–Jul), Rabi (Oct–Nov). Irrigate at knee-high, tasseling, silking. 120N, 60P, 40K split.
    4.          
  4. Soybean: Kharif—last week Jun to mid-Jul. Use lifeline irrigation, 30–40N, 60–80P, 30–40K, rhizobium inoculation.
    5.          
  5. Chickpea: Rabi—Oct to mid-Nov. Rainfed, can irrigate if possible at flowering/pod-filling. 20–25N, 40–60P.
    6.          
  6. Mustard: Rabi—mid-Oct to early Nov. Water at branching, flowering, pod stage. 80–120N, 40–60P, 40K.
    7.          
  7. Cotton: Kharif—May/June (NW), June/July (South/Central). Avoid waterlogging! 60–75N, 30–40P, 30–40K. Harvest Oct–Jan.
    8.          
  8. Potato (Rabi/plains): Sow Oct/Nov, 7–9 irrigations, 120N, 80P, 120K. Hills: plant March–May.
    9.        
     
     
          
              Fertilizer & Pest Advisory         Soil-based recommendations, early pest warnings.            
       

Pest & Fertilizer Guidance (2025)

       

Key Principle: Fertilizer dose and timings (N:P:K and micronutrients) depend on your region, crop, variety, and current soil test. Use soil health card results where available!

       
             
  • Rice: 90–120N, 40–60P, 40–60K per ha. Split 1/2 basal, 1/4 at tillering, 1/4 panicle stage; monitor leaf folder, blast, sheath blight, stem borer.
    •          
  • Wheat: 120–150N, 60P, 40K per ha; top-dress after irrigation; watch rusts, aphids, armyworm.
    •          
  • Cotton: 60–90N, 30–45P, 30–45K in 2–3 splits; beware of bollworm, jassid, whitefly.
    •          
  • Soybean: 30–40N, 60–80P, 30–40K per ha; healthy nodulation by rhizobium; monitor pod borers.
    •          
  • Vegetables (e.g., Tomato): 100–120N, 60–80P, 60–80K per ha plus Ca, B, organic matter if possible.
    •          
  • Pulse Crops: 20N starter, 40–60P, watch borers.
    •        
       

Common Pest Outbreaks (2025)

       
             
  • Fall Armyworm: Maize, sorghum. Use pheromone traps, rotate crops, biopesticide sprays.
    •          
  • Bollworm: Cotton, tomato. Use Bt strategy, pheromone lures, rotate chemicals.
    •          
  • Aphids: Veg & soybeans. Biological (wasps), minimize heavy liquid insecticides.
    •          
  • Thrips: Chilli, capsicum, onion. Yellow traps, neem + IPM, rotate chemistries.
    •          
  • Green Leafhopper/Jassid: Cotton. Vigilant monitoring, regional advisories, quick action if needed.
    •          
  • Mango pests: Andhra coastal, 2025. Timely insecticides, field hygiene, community approach.
    •        
       

General Notes

       
             
  • Always follow integrated pest management (IPM), use rotation, keep field clean.
    •          
  • Adjust fertilizer for your soil test, last crop, and organic matter.
    •          
  • Spray only with confirmed pest presence (Economic Threshold Levels).
    •

Soil & Weather-based Crop Choices

       
             
  • Sandy Soils (Rajasthan, Haryana, Gujarat): Bajra, mung, moth, groundnut, cumin, watermelon. Opt for drought-tolerant and quick-growing Kharif varieties.
    •          
  • Black Cotton Soils (Maharashtra, MP, TS, AP, Gujarat): Cotton, soybean, tur, sorghum, chickpea, sunflower, maize, wheat (with irrigation). Go for crops needing higher moisture-retention, especially after monsoon.
    •          
  • Red/Loamy Soils: (TN, Karnataka, E. India): Millets, pulses, groundnut, castor, ragi, sugarcane, vegetables. Diversified cropping and moisture conservation work well.
    •          
  • Alluvial Soils (Punjab, Haryana, UP, Bihar, West Bengal, Assam): Rice (Kharif), wheat (Rabi), sugarcane, maize, jute, potato, pulses, mustard, vegetables. Manage water to avoid waterlogging.
    •          
  • Laterite Soils (Kerala, Karnataka, Maharashtra, WB hills): Cashew, tea, coffee, rubber, pineapple, millets, pulses, coconut. Use more organics, consider shredded mulch, slopes.
    •        
       

Example Combo Strategies:

       
             
  • Rajasthan: Sandy, low-rain: Bajra, mung, moth (Kharif), gram, mustard (Rabi).
    •          
  • Punjab/Haryana: Alluvial, irrigated: Rice/wheat, sugarcane, potato.
    •          
  • Maharashtra: Cotton, soybean, jowar, tur (Kharif); Rabi IRRIGATED.
    •          
  • West Bengal: Paddy, jute, vegetables (Kharif); wheat, tea, potato (Rabi).
    •          
  • Tamil Nadu: Red loam, humid: Groundnut, millets (Kharif), rice w/ irrigation.
    •        
       

Advice: Use the Soil Health Card service to get custom nutrient/fertilizer guidance. Adapt crop schedule to local rainfall and soil holding capacity for better yield and risk reduction.
